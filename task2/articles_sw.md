# Selected Wookieepedia Articles (40 key entities)

## 1. Luke Skywalker
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Luke_Skywalker
- **Content:**
Luke Skywalker was a legendary Jedi Master who was instrumental in defeating the Galactic Empire and the Sith. Born in 19 BBY as the son of Jedi Knight Anakin Skywalker and Senator Padmé Amidala, he was, along with his twin sister Leia, one of the most powerful Force‑sensitives in the galaxy. After his father’s fall to the dark side and the rise of the Empire, he was taken to the desert world Tatooine and raised as a farm boy by his aunt and uncle, Beru and Owen Lars. The Empire’s pursuit of the droids C-3PO and R2-D2 set him on a collision course with destiny, leading him to join the Alliance to Restore the Republic. Trained by Jedi Masters Obi‑Wan Kenobi and Yoda, he became a formidable Jedi Knight and a symbol of hope for the Rebellion. His unshakable faith in the good within his father ultimately led to Anakin’s redemption and the destruction of the Sith. Following the Empire’s fall, he attempted to rebuild the Jedi Order, but a moment of weakness with his nephew, Ben Solo, drove him into self‑exile. In his final years, he projected himself across the galaxy to save the Resistance, becoming one with the Force and inspiring a new generation of heroes.

## 2. Darth Vader
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Darth_Vader
- **Content:**
Darth Vader, also known as Anakin Skywalker, was a Dark Lord of the Sith and the central figure of the Galactic Empire. Once a celebrated Jedi Knight prophesied to bring balance to the Force, he was seduced by the dark side and became Emperor Palpatine’s enforcer. His body was grievously burned in a duel with Obi‑Wan Kenobi on Mustafar, forcing him into a black suit of armor that sustained his shattered form. As the Empire’s supreme commander, he led the hunt for the Jedi, crushed dissent across the galaxy, and served as a terrifying symbol of Imperial might. Despite his turn to evil, the love for his son, Luke Skywalker, ultimately pulled him back to the light. In his final moments, he fulfilled the prophecy by killing Palpatine and sacrificing himself to save his son, restoring balance to the Force.

## 3. Princess Leia Organa
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Leia_Skywalker_Organa
- **Content:**
Leia Skywalker Organa, born Leia Amidala Skywalker, was a Force‑sensitive human female who served as a princess of Alderaan, a general of the Rebel Alliance, and a founder of the Resistance. The daughter of Padmé Amidala and Anakin Skywalker, she was separated from her twin brother Luke at birth and adopted by Senator Bail Organa and Queen Breha of Alderaan. Leia became a key leader in the fight against the Galactic Empire, serving as a member of the Imperial Senate while secretly aiding the rebellion. She was instrumental in obtaining the Death Star plans, which led to the station’s destruction. After Alderaan’s annihilation by the Death Star, she dedicated herself fully to the Alliance, eventually rising to the rank of general. Following the war, she continued to serve the New Republic, but later founded the Resistance to counter the rise of the First Order. Throughout her life, she was a skilled diplomat, military strategist, and, in her later years, a Jedi apprentice, though she ultimately chose a path of political leadership.

## 4. Han Solo
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Han_Solo
- **Content:**
Han Solo was a human male Corellian smuggler, captain of the Millennium Falcon, and a general in the Rebel Alliance. Born on Corellia, he escaped a life of crime with his childhood friend Qi’ra, eventually becoming a smuggler under the tutelage of Tobias Beckett. He won the Millennium Falcon from Lando Calrissian in a sabacc game and gained notoriety for making the Kessel Run in less than twelve parsecs. After meeting Luke Skywalker and Obi‑Wan Kenobi, he joined the Rebellion, becoming a pivotal figure in the Battle of Yavin and later the Battle of Endor. His romance with Princess Leia Organa blossomed into marriage, and they had a son, Ben. Han’s roguish charm, piloting skill, and moral evolution from a cynical smuggler to a heroic general made him a legendary figure in galactic history. He was killed by his son, Kylo Ren, but his legacy endured.

## 5. Obi‑Wan Kenobi
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi
- **Content:**
Obi‑Wan Kenobi was a legendary Jedi Master who played a pivotal role in the downfall of the Galactic Empire. Born in 57 BBY, he was apprenticed to Qui‑Gon Jinn and later trained Anakin Skywalker, whom he believed to be the Chosen One. After Anakin’s fall to the dark side, Obi‑Wan defeated him in a lightsaber duel on Mustafar and went into hiding on Tatooine, watching over Luke Skywalker from afar. He emerged from exile to guide Luke, sacrificing himself in a duel with Darth Vader aboard the first Death Star. His spirit became one with the Force, continuing to counsel Luke from beyond death. Obi‑Wan’s dedication to the Jedi way and his unwavering commitment to protecting the Skywalker family made him one of the most respected Jedi in history.

## 6. Yoda
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Yoda
- **Content:**
Yoda was a legendary Jedi Master who served as the Grand Master of the Jedi Order for centuries. Born around 896 BBY, he was a member of a mysterious species and was renowned for his wisdom, combat prowess, and deep connection to the Force. He trained countless Jedi, including Count Dooku, Mace Windu, and later, Luke Skywalker. Following the rise of the Galactic Empire, Yoda went into exile on Dagobah, where he lived in solitude until Luke sought his tutelage. Though he initially hesitated to train Luke due to his age and recklessness, he ultimately imparted the essential teachings of the Jedi. Before his death at the age of 900, he revealed the truth about Luke’s parentage and passed into the Force, becoming a guiding spirit.

## 7. R2-D2
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/R2-D2
- **Content:**
R2-D2, often called Artoo, was an R2-series astromech droid who served numerous masters, including Padmé Amidala, Anakin Skywalker, and Princess Leia Organa, but was most famously associated with Luke Skywalker and the Rebel Alliance. Throughout the Galactic Civil War, Artoo played a crucial role in critical missions: carrying the Death Star plans, storing the holographic message from Leia, and helping shut down the shield generator on Endor. With his companion C-3PO, Artoo witnessed nearly every major event in the history of the galaxy, from the fall of the Republic to the rise of the First Order. His bravery, resourcefulness, and unwavering loyalty made him one of the most celebrated droids in galactic history.

## 8. C-3PO
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/C-3PO
- **Content:**
C-3PO, also known as Threepio, was a humanoid protocol droid fluent in over seven million forms of communication. Built by Anakin Skywalker on Tatooine, he served alongside R2-D2 through countless adventures. Threepio was often seen as fussy and anxious, but his loyalty to his masters—Padmé Amidala, Bail Organa, Leia Organa, and Luke Skywalker—was unwavering. He played a key role in diplomatic missions and was present at many historic events, from the Battle of Geonosis to the Battle of Endor and beyond. Despite his lack of combat abilities, his linguistic skills and encyclopedic knowledge made him indispensable to the Rebel Alliance and later the Resistance.

## 9. Chewbacca
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Chewbacca
- **Content:**
Chewbacca, known affectionately as Chewie, was a legendary Wookiee warrior from Kashyyyk and the loyal co‑pilot of the Millennium Falcon. He fought alongside Han Solo for decades, first as a smuggler and then as a general in the Rebel Alliance. Chewbacca’s strength, skill with a bowcaster, and fierce loyalty made him a vital member of the Rebellion; he participated in key battles including the Battle of Yavin, the Battle of Hoth, and the Battle of Endor. After Han’s death, he continued the fight with the Resistance, mentoring Rey and carrying on Han’s legacy. He was a symbol of Wookiee courage and remained one of the most respected figures in the galaxy.

## 10. Palpatine
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Palpatine
- **Content:**
Sheev Palpatine, also known as Darth Sidious, was a Dark Lord of the Sith and the Emperor of the Galactic Empire. Born on Naboo, he rose through the political ranks to become Supreme Chancellor, manipulating the Separatist crisis and the Clone Wars to consolidate power. As Darth Sidious, he orchestrated the destruction of the Jedi Order and transformed the Republic into a tyrannical Empire. He ruled for decades, using the dark side to extend his life and maintain control. After being apparently killed by Darth Vader, he secretly returned using Sith cloning rituals, seeking to reclaim his dominion. His legacy was one of absolute evil, yet he came close to achieving ultimate power over the galaxy.

## 11. Millennium Falcon
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Millennium_Falcon
- **Content:**
The Millennium Falcon was a legendary YT-1300 light freighter that served a number of owners, including Lando Calrissian and Han Solo. It was renowned for its speed, maneuverability, and extensive modifications that made it one of the fastest ships in the galaxy, capable of making the Kessel Run in less than twelve parsecs. During the Galactic Civil War, the Falcon became a symbol of the Rebellion, participating in the destruction of the first Death Star, the evacuation of Hoth, and the mission to Endor that led to the fall of the Empire. After Han Solo’s death, the ship fell into the hands of Rey, who continued to use it in the fight against the First Order.

## 12. Death Star
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Star
- **Content:**
The Death Star was a moon-sized battle station armed with a superlaser capable of destroying entire planets. Conceived by the Geonosians and constructed in secret by the Galactic Empire, it was designed to enforce the Tarkin Doctrine through fear. The first Death Star was destroyed by Luke Skywalker at the Battle of Yavin, but a second, larger Death Star was later built, only to be destroyed during the Battle of Endor. Both stations became symbols of Imperial tyranny and were key targets of the Rebel Alliance.

## 13. Lightsaber
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Lightsaber
- **Content:**
A lightsaber was a weapon used by the Jedi, the Sith, and other Force‑sensitive individuals. It consisted of a plasma blade powered by a kyber crystal, emitted from a metal hilt. The blade could cut through almost any material and deflect blaster bolts. Lightsabers came in various colors, with blue, green, and purple being common among Jedi, and red among the Sith. The construction of a lightsaber was a rite of passage for Jedi Padawans. The weapon was both a symbol of the Jedi Order and a deadly tool in the hands of a skilled wielder.

## 14. The Force
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/The_Force
- **Content:**
The Force was a mystical, ubiquitous energy field generated by all living things that bound the galaxy together. It could be harnessed by those sensitive to it, granting abilities such as telekinesis, precognition, and enhanced physical prowess. The Force had a light side, associated with selflessness and compassion, and a dark side, fueled by fear, anger, and aggression. The Jedi Order sought to follow the light side and maintain balance, while the Sith embraced the dark side to gain power.

## 15. Jedi
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Jedi
- **Content:**
The Jedi were an ancient order of Force‑sensitive guardians who served the Galactic Republic for millennia. They dedicated themselves to the light side of the Force, acting as peacekeepers, diplomats, and warriors. The Jedi Order was nearly destroyed by Order 66, which branded them as traitors to the Empire. Survivors went into hiding, and the Order was eventually restored by Luke Skywalker and later by Rey. Jedi were known for their distinctive robes, lightsabers, and commitment to a code that emphasized serenity, knowledge, and harmony.

## 16. Sith
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Sith
- **Content:**
The Sith were an ancient order of Force‑sensitive practitioners who wielded the dark side. Emerging as a splinter faction of the Jedi, they sought power and domination, often operating through manipulation and secrecy. The Sith followed the Rule of Two, established by Darth Bane, which limited their numbers to a master and an apprentice to concentrate power. The most famous Sith Lords were Darth Sidious (Palpatine) and Darth Vader, who orchestrated the fall of the Republic and the rise of the Galactic Empire.

## 17. Tatooine
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Tatooine
- **Content:**
Tatooine was a desert planet orbiting twin suns in the Outer Rim Territories. It was a harsh world known for its moisture farms, criminal syndicates, and slave trade. Tatooine was the home of Anakin Skywalker and Luke Skywalker, and it served as a key location in many pivotal events, including the beginning of the Skywalker saga and the rise of the Rebel Alliance. Major settlements included Mos Eisley, Mos Espa, and Anchorhead.

## 18. Coruscant
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Coruscant
- **Content:**
Coruscant was an ecumenopolis—a planet‑wide city—located in the Core Worlds. It served as the capital of the Galactic Republic, the Galactic Empire, the New Republic, and later the First Order. Coruscant was the political and cultural heart of the galaxy, housing the Jedi Temple, the Senate Building, and countless districts ranging from opulent to squalid. Its history spanned millennia, and it was the site of numerous crucial events, including Palpatine’s rise to power and the Empire’s proclamation.

## 19. The Galactic Empire
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Galactic_Empire
- **Content:**
The Galactic Empire, also known as the First Galactic Empire, was the authoritarian regime that replaced the Galactic Republic at the end of the Clone Wars. Proclaimed by Supreme Chancellor Palpatine, who became Emperor, it enforced control through military might, the Tarkin Doctrine, and the eradication of the Jedi Order. The Empire ruled for approximately two decades before being overthrown by the Rebel Alliance, though remnants later reformed as the First Order.

## 20. The Rebel Alliance
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Rebel_Alliance
- **Content:**
The Rebel Alliance, formally the Alliance to Restore the Republic, was a resistance movement formed to oppose the tyranny of the Galactic Empire. Composed of defectors, planetary militias, and freedom fighters, it waged a guerrilla war against Imperial forces. The Alliance achieved its greatest victory at the Battle of Endor, which led to the Empire’s collapse. Its leaders included Mon Mothma, Bail Organa, and Princess Leia Organa, and its heroes included Luke Skywalker, Han Solo, and many others who became legends.

## 21. Naboo
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Naboo
- **Content:**
Naboo was a pastoral planet located in the Mid Rim, known for its stunning natural beauty, rolling plains, and swampy lake country. It was the homeworld of Padmé Amidala and later Emperor Palpatine. The planet was inhabited by two species: the human Naboo and the amphibious Gungans, who lived in underwater cities. Naboo was the site of the Trade Federation’s invasion in 32 BBY, which led to the rise of Palpatine and the start of the Clone Wars. Its capital, Theed, was a center of art and culture. Despite the Empire’s rise, Naboo remained a symbol of resistance and resilience.

## 22. Hoth
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Hoth
- **Content:**
Hoth was a remote ice planet in the Outer Rim Territories, home to native creatures such as wampas and tauntauns. It served as the location of Echo Base, a secret Rebel Alliance outpost during the Galactic Civil War. In 3 ABY, the Empire discovered the base and launched a massive ground assault, leading to the Battle of Hoth. The battle forced the Rebels to evacuate, though they suffered heavy losses. Hoth’s harsh environment made it a difficult place to sustain long-term operations, and it was abandoned by both factions after the battle.

## 23. Endor
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Endor
- **Content:**
Endor, also known as the Forest Moon of Endor, was a small, forested moon orbiting the gas giant Endor. It was home to the Ewoks, a species of small, primitive ursinoids. The moon became the site of a pivotal battle in 4 ABY when the Rebel Alliance launched a mission to destroy the second Death Star, which was under construction in orbit. The Battle of Endor resulted in the destruction of the second Death Star, the deaths of Emperor Palpatine and Darth Vader, and the collapse of the Galactic Empire. The moon was later protected as a sanctuary by the New Republic.

## 24. Kashyyyk
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Kashyyyk
- **Content:**
Kashyyyk was a forest planet in the Mid Rim, home to the Wookiees. Covered in massive wroshyr trees, the planet’s cities were built among the branches. Kashyyyk was invaded by the Separatists during the Clone Wars, and later, after the rise of the Empire, the Wookiees were enslaved and used for labor. The planet was liberated by the Rebellion, and Wookiee warriors, including Chewbacca, played crucial roles in the fight against the Empire. Kashyyyk remained a symbol of freedom and resilience.

## 25. Alderaan
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Alderaan
- **Content:**
Alderaan was a peaceful planet in the Core Worlds, known for its commitment to diplomacy, art, and environmentalism. It was the homeworld of Princess Leia Organa and Senator Bail Organa. Alderaan was a founding member of the Galactic Republic and later a key supporter of the Rebel Alliance. In 0 BBY, Grand Moff Tarkin ordered the destruction of Alderaan by the Death Star as a demonstration of Imperial power, killing all its inhabitants. The destruction of Alderaan galvanized the Rebellion and became a symbol of the Empire’s tyranny.

## 26. Dagobah
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Dagobah
- **Content:**
Dagobah was a swampy, remote planet in the Dagobah system. Strong with the Force, it served as the hiding place of Jedi Master Yoda after the fall of the Republic. Yoda lived in exile there for nearly two decades, until Luke Skywalker arrived to train as a Jedi. The planet was filled with dangerous creatures and a dark side cave that tested those who entered. Dagobah’s isolation and dense vegetation made it an ideal refuge for a fugitive Jedi.

## 27. Bespin
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Bespin
- **Content:**
Bespin was a gas giant in the Anoat sector, known for its tibanna gas mining operations. The planet was orbited by floating cities, most famously Cloud City, which was governed by Lando Calrissian. In 3 ABY, Cloud City became the site of a confrontation between Darth Vader, Luke Skywalker, and Han Solo, where Han was frozen in carbonite and Luke learned that Vader was his father. Bespin’s economy relied heavily on tibanna gas exports, making it a strategic asset.

## 28. Kamino
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Kamino
- **Content:**
Kamino was an aquatic planet located far beyond the Outer Rim, hidden from galactic records. Its cities were built on stilts above the stormy oceans. Kamino was the home of the Kaminoans, a species of tall, slender beings known for their expertise in cloning. The planet was where the Grand Army of the Republic was created, using the genetic template of Jango Fett. Kamino was removed from the Jedi Archives by a conspirator, keeping its existence secret until Obi-Wan Kenobi discovered it. During the Clone Wars, Kamino was attacked by Separatist forces, but the Republic defended it. After the Empire rose, cloning facilities were expanded, but Kamino eventually rebelled against Imperial control.

## 29. Geonosis
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Geonosis
- **Content:**
Geonosis was a rocky desert planet in the Outer Rim, home to the insectoid Geonosians. It was the site of the first major battle of the Clone Wars, where the Jedi and the newly formed Grand Army of the Republic fought the Separatist droid army. Geonosis also housed massive droid foundries and was where the Death Star was initially designed. After the Clone Wars, the Empire sterilized much of the planet’s surface to eliminate the Geonosian species. Ruins of the droid factories remained.

## 30. Mustafar
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Mustafar
- **Content:**
Mustafar was a volcanic planet in the Outer Rim, covered in lava rivers and active volcanoes. It was the site of a Separatist stronghold during the Clone Wars, and after the war, Darth Vader was defeated by Obi-Wan Kenobi there, suffering catastrophic injuries that led to his transformation into a cyborg. Later, Vader established a fortress on Mustafar, using it as a base for his dark side activities. The planet’s harsh environment made it a place of pain and power for the Sith.

## 31. TIE Fighter
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/TIE_fighter
- **Content:**
The TIE fighter, short for Twin Ion Engine fighter, was a series of starfighters used by the Galactic Empire. Designed for speed and maneuverability, TIE fighters lacked hyperdrive and shields, relying on swarm tactics to overwhelm enemies. The standard TIE/ln model was the most common starfighter in the Imperial Navy, seen at major battles including Yavin and Endor. Variants included the TIE interceptor, TIE bomber, and TIE advanced. After the Empire’s fall, TIE fighters continued to serve the First Order and other successor factions.

## 32. Star Destroyer
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Star_Destroyer
- **Content:**
A Star Destroyer was a dagger-shaped capital ship used by the Galactic Empire as a symbol of naval power. The Imperial-class Star Destroyer measured 1,600 meters in length and carried over 37,000 crew, 72 TIE fighters, and ground assault forces. These vessels were the backbone of Imperial fleet operations, enforcing planetary blockades and engaging in orbital bombardments. Larger dreadnoughts like the Executor-class Super Star Destroyer served as flagships for commanders such as Darth Vader. Following the Empire’s collapse, Star Destroyer designs influenced the First Order’s Resurgent-class battlecruisers.

## 33. AT-AT
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/AT-AT
- **Content:**
The All Terrain Armored Transport (AT-AT) was a quadrupedal walker used by the Galactic Empire for ground assault and planetary invasion. Standing over 22 meters tall, the AT-AT was heavily armored and equipped with laser cannons. It was first deployed at the Battle of Hoth, where it overwhelmed the Rebel Alliance’s defenses. Despite its imposing presence, AT-ATs were vulnerable to harpoon-and-tow-cable tactics. Variants included the AT-ACT cargo walker and the later AT-M6 used by the First Order.

## 34. Order 66
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Order_66
- **Content:**
Order 66 was a classified contingency order given to the clone troopers of the Grand Army of the Republic. It declared the Jedi Order traitors to the Republic and authorized their summary execution. The order was activated by Supreme Chancellor Palpatine at the end of the Clone Wars, leading to the near-total extermination of the Jedi. Order 66 marked the fall of the Jedi Order and the transformation of the Republic into the Galactic Empire. Only a handful of Jedi, including Obi‑Wan Kenobi and Yoda, survived.

## 35. Battle of Yavin
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_Yavin
- **Content:**
The Battle of Yavin was a major conflict in the Galactic Civil War that resulted in the destruction of the first Death Star. It took place near the gas giant Yavin, where the Rebel Alliance launched a desperate assault against the Empire’s superweapon. Using proton torpedoes fired into a small exhaust port, Luke Skywalker destroyed the battle station, achieving a decisive victory for the Rebellion. The battle marked the first major defeat of the Empire and established Luke as a hero of the Alliance.

## 36. Battle of Endor
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_Endor
- **Content:**
The Battle of Endor was the climactic battle of the Galactic Civil War, fought in 4 ABY around the Forest Moon of Endor and the second Death Star. The Rebel Alliance launched a combined space and ground assault to destroy the Death Star and defeat the Emperor. While the space battle raged, a strike team led by Han Solo destroyed the shield generator protecting the Death Star. Inside the station, Darth Vader redeemed himself by killing Emperor Palpatine. The destruction of the Death Star and the deaths of Palpatine and Vader led to the collapse of the Galactic Empire.

## 37. The Clone Wars
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Clone_Wars
- **Content:**
The Clone Wars were a galaxy‑wide conflict fought between the Galactic Republic and the Confederacy of Independent Systems (Separatists). Lasting three years (22–19 BBY), the war saw the deployment of clone troopers against droid armies. The conflict was secretly orchestrated by Darth Sidious to destabilize the Republic, consolidate power, and eliminate the Jedi Order. Key battles included Geonosis, Christophsis, and Coruscant. The war ended with Order 66 and the rise of the Galactic Empire.

## 38. Kylo Ren
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Kylo_Ren
- **Content:**
Kylo Ren, born Ben Solo, was a Force‑sensitive human who served as the Supreme Leader of the First Order. The son of Han Solo and Leia Organa, and the nephew of Luke Skywalker, he was trained as a Jedi but was seduced by the dark side by Snoke. He adopted the name Kylo Ren and became a master of the Knights of Ren. Torn between light and dark, he ultimately killed his father, but later redeemed himself by saving Rey from Palpatine, giving his life to restore balance to the Force.

## 39. Rey
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Rey
- **Content:**
Rey was a Force‑sensitive human who rose from a life as a scavenger on Jakku to become the last Jedi. Initially drawn into the conflict between the Resistance and the First Order, she discovered her latent Force abilities and sought training from Luke Skywalker. Rey eventually confronted Emperor Palpatine, learning she was his granddaughter. Rejecting her dark lineage, she embraced the light and, with the help of redeemed Ben Solo, defeated Palpatine. After the war, she took the name Rey Skywalker to honor her mentors and began rebuilding the Jedi Order.

## 40. Separatist Senate
- **Link:** https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Separatist_Senate
- **Content:**
The Separatist Senate, also known as the Separatist Parliament, the Legislative Senate, the Confederate parliament or the Separatist Congress, was the civilian legislative body of the Confederacy of Independent Systems, representing thousands of its member worlds during the Clone Wars, and consisting of the senators who represented Separatist-aligned worlds. When the charismatic Count Dooku founded the Confederacy in 24 BBY, the swaths of sectors and star systems seceding from the Galactic Republic formed their own congressional assembly to oversee the new Separatist state. A number of the appointed delegates, many having served as members of the Galactic Senate prior to their secession, continued to represent their constituents in the Confederacy. Operating by a written constitution known as the Bylaws of Independent Systems, the Senate was headed by the Confederate Head of State, Dooku, as well as by Separatist Congress Leader Bec Lawise. During the duration of the Clone Wars, the congressional body was headquartered on the Confederate capital of Raxus Secundus, where it regularly convened for sessions held in the Separatist Senate Building.
