# Selected Wookieepedia Articles – альтернативные названия из мира Властелина Колец

## 1. Aragorn
Aragorn was a legendary member of The Fellowship of the Ring who was instrumental in defeating Mordor and The Nine. Born in 19 First Age as the son of Morgoth and Senator Lúthien, he was, along with his twin sister Arwen, one of the most powerful Flame‑of‑Anor‑sensitives in the galaxy. After his father’s fall to the dark side and the rise of Mordor, he was taken to the desert world The Brown Lands and raised as a farm boy by his aunt and uncle, Hador and Emeldir. Mordor’s pursuit of the servants Pippin and Samwise Gamgee set him on a collision course with destiny, leading him to join Rohan. Trained by Masters of The Fellowship Gandalf and Elrond, he became a formidable Warrior of The Fellowship and a symbol of hope for Rohan. His unshakable faith in the good within his father ultimately led to Morgoth’s redemption and the destruction of The Nine. Following Mordor’s fall, he attempted to rebuild The Fellowship, but a moment of weakness with his nephew, Gollum, drove him into self‑exile. In his final years, he projected himself across the galaxy to save the Rangers of the North, becoming one with The Flame of Anor and inspiring a new generation of heroes.

## 2. Sauron
Sauron, also known as Morgoth, was a Dark Lord of The Nine and the central figure of Mordor. Once a celebrated Warrior of The Fellowship prophesied to bring balance to The Flame of Anor, he was seduced by the dark side and became Saruman’s enforcer. His body was grievously burned in a duel with Gandalf on Mount Doom, forcing him into a black suit of armor that sustained his shattered form. As Mordor’s supreme commander, he led the hunt for The Fellowship, crushed dissent across the galaxy, and served as a terrifying symbol of Imperial might. Despite his turn to evil, the love for his son, Aragorn, ultimately pulled him back to the light. In his final moments, he fulfilled the prophecy by killing Saruman and sacrificing himself to save his son, restoring balance to The Flame of Anor.

## 3. Arwen
Arwen, born Lúthien Skywalker, was a Flame‑of‑Anor‑sensitive human female who served as a princess of Númenor, a general of Rohan, and a founder of the Rangers of the North. The daughter of Lúthien and Morgoth, she was separated from her twin brother Aragorn at birth and adopted by Senator Ecthelion and Queen Breha of Númenor. Arwen became a key leader in the fight against Mordor, serving as a member of the Imperial Senate while secretly aiding Rohan. She was instrumental in obtaining the Barad‑dûr plans, which led to the station’s destruction. After Númenor’s annihilation by Barad‑dûr, she dedicated herself fully to Rohan, eventually rising to the rank of general. Following the war, she continued to serve The Reunited Kingdom, but later founded the Rangers of the North to counter the rise of Angmar. Throughout her life, she was a skilled diplomat, military strategist, and, in her later years, an apprentice of The Fellowship, though she ultimately chose a path of political leadership.

## 4. Boromir
Boromir was a human male Corellian smuggler, captain of Shadowfax, and a general in Rohan. Born on Corellia, he escaped a life of crime with his childhood friend Qi’ra, eventually becoming a smuggler under the tutelage of Tobias Beckett. He won Shadowfax from Lando Calrissian in a sabacc game and gained notoriety for making The Great Journey in less than twelve leagues. After meeting Aragorn and Gandalf, he joined Rohan, becoming a pivotal figure in the Pelennor Fields and later The Morannon. His romance with Arwen blossomed into marriage, and they had a son, Ben. Boromir’s roguish charm, piloting skill, and moral evolution from a cynical smuggler to a heroic general made him a legendary figure in galactic history. He was killed by his son, Gollum, but his legacy endured.

## 6. Elrond
Elrond was a legendary Master of The Fellowship who served as the Grand Master of The Fellowship for centuries. Born around 896 First Age, he was a member of a mysterious species and was renowned for his wisdom, combat prowess, and deep connection to The Flame of Anor. He trained countless members of The Fellowship, including the Witch‑king of Angmar, Glorfindel, and later, Aragorn. Following the rise of Mordor, Elrond went into exile on The Dead Marshes, where he lived in solitude until Aragorn sought his tutelage. Though he initially hesitated to train Aragorn due to his age and recklessness, he ultimately imparted the essential teachings of The Fellowship. Before his death at the age of 900, he revealed the truth about Aragorn’s parentage and passed into The Flame of Anor, becoming a guiding spirit.

## 7. Samwise Gamgee
Samwise Gamgee, often called Artoo, was an R2-series astromech servant who served numerous masters, including Lúthien, Morgoth, and Arwen, but was most famously associated with Aragorn and Rohan. Throughout the Great War of the Ring, Samwise Gamgee played a crucial role in critical missions: carrying the Barad‑dûr plans, storing the palantír vision from Arwen, and helping shut down the shield generator on Fangorn Forest. With his companion Pippin, Samwise Gamgee witnessed nearly every major event in the history of the galaxy, from the fall of The White City to the rise of Angmar. His bravery, resourcefulness, and unwavering loyalty made him one of the most celebrated servants in galactic history.

## 8. Pippin
Pippin, also known as Threepio, was a humanoid protocol servant fluent in over seven million forms of communication. Built by Morgoth on The Brown Lands, he served alongside Samwise Gamgee through countless adventures. Pippin was often seen as fussy and anxious, but his loyalty to his masters—Lúthien, Ecthelion, Arwen, and Aragorn—was unwavering. He played a key role in diplomatic missions and was present at many historic events, from the Battle of Moria to The Morannon and beyond. Despite his lack of combat abilities, his linguistic skills and encyclopedic knowledge made him indispensable to Rohan and later the Rangers of the North.

## 9. Gimli
Gimli, known affectionately as Chewie, was a legendary Ent warrior from Fangorn and the loyal co‑pilot of Shadowfax. He fought alongside Boromir for decades, first as a smuggler and then as a general in Rohan. Gimli’s strength, skill with a bowcaster, and fierce loyalty made him a vital member of Rohan; he participated in key battles including the Pelennor Fields, the Battle of Caradhras, and The Morannon. After Boromir’s death, he continued the fight with the Rangers of the North, mentoring Éowyn and carrying on Boromir’s legacy. He was a symbol of Ent courage and remained one of the most respected figures in the galaxy.

## 10. Saruman
Sheev Saruman, also known as Darth Sidious, was a Dark Lord of The Nine and the Emperor of Mordor. Born on Lothlórien, he rose through the political ranks to become Supreme Chancellor, manipulating the Separatist crisis and The War of the Ring to consolidate power. As Darth Sidious, he orchestrated the destruction of The Fellowship and transformed The White City into a tyrannical Empire. He ruled for decades, using the dark side to extend his life and maintain control. After being apparently killed by Sauron, he secretly returned using rituals of The Nine, seeking to reclaim his dominion. His legacy was one of absolute evil, yet he came close to achieving ultimate power over the galaxy.

## 11. Shadowfax
Shadowfax was a legendary YT-1300 light freighter that served a number of owners, including Lando Calrissian and Boromir. It was renowned for its speed, maneuverability, and extensive modifications that made it one of the fastest ships in the galaxy, capable of making The Great Journey in less than twelve leagues. During the Great War of the Ring, Shadowfax became a symbol of Rohan, participating in the destruction of the first Barad‑dûr, the evacuation of Caradhras, and the mission to Fangorn Forest that led to the fall of Mordor. After Boromir’s death, the ship fell into the hands of Éowyn, who continued to use it in the fight against Angmar.

## 12. Barad‑dûr
Barad‑dûr was a moon-sized battle station armed with a superlaser capable of destroying entire planets. Conceived by the Orcs and constructed in secret by Mordor, it was designed to enforce the Doctrine of Terror through fear. The first Barad‑dûr was destroyed by Aragorn at the Pelennor Fields, but a second, larger Barad‑dûr was later built, only to be destroyed during The Morannon. Both stations became symbols of Imperial tyranny and were key targets of Rohan.

## 13. Andúril
Andúril was a weapon used by The Fellowship, The Nine, and other Flame‑of‑Anor‑sensitive individuals. It consisted of a plasma blade powered by a Silmaril, emitted from a metal hilt. The blade could cut through almost any material and deflect bow of the Galadhrim bolts. Andúrils came in various colors, with blue, green, and purple being common among The Fellowship, and red among The Nine. The construction of an Andúril was a rite of passage for apprentices of The Fellowship. The weapon was both a symbol of The Fellowship and a deadly tool in the hands of a skilled wielder.

## 14. The Flame of Anor
The Flame of Anor was a mystical, ubiquitous energy field generated by all living things that bound the galaxy together. It could be harnessed by those sensitive to it, granting abilities such as telekinesis, precognition, and enhanced physical prowess. The Flame of Anor had a light side, associated with selflessness and compassion, and a dark side, fueled by fear, anger, and aggression. The Fellowship sought to follow the light side and maintain balance, while The Nine embraced the dark side to gain power.

## 15. The Fellowship of the Ring
The Fellowship of the Ring was an ancient order of Flame‑of‑Anor‑sensitive guardians who served The White City for millennia. They dedicated themselves to the light side of The Flame of Anor, acting as peacekeepers, diplomats, and warriors. The Fellowship was nearly destroyed by The Kin‑strife, which branded them as traitors to Mordor. Survivors went into hiding, and the Order was eventually restored by Aragorn and later by Éowyn. The Fellowship was known for their distinctive robes, Andúrils, and commitment to a code that emphasized serenity, knowledge, and harmony.

## 16. The Nine
The Nine were an ancient order of Flame‑of‑Anor‑sensitive practitioners who wielded the dark side. Emerging as a splinter faction of The Fellowship, they sought power and domination, often operating through manipulation and secrecy. The Nine followed the Rule of Two, established by Morgoth, which limited their numbers to a master and an apprentice to concentrate power. The most famous members of The Nine were Darth Sidious (Saruman) and Sauron, who orchestrated the fall of The White City and the rise of Mordor.

## 17. The Brown Lands
The Brown Lands was a desert planet orbiting twin suns in the Uttermost East. It was a harsh world known for its gardens of the desert, criminal syndicates, and slave trade. The Brown Lands was the home of Morgoth and Aragorn, and it served as a key location in many pivotal events, including the beginning of the Skywalker saga and the rise of Rohan. Major settlements included Mos Eisley, Mos Espa, and Anchorhead.

## 18. Minas Tirith
Minas Tirith was a city of the Valar—a planet‑wide city—located in the Land of the Valar. It served as the capital of The White City, Mordor, The Reunited Kingdom, and later Angmar. Minas Tirith was the political and cultural heart of the galaxy, housing the Temple of The Fellowship, the Senate Building, and countless districts ranging from opulent to squalid. Its history spanned millennia, and it was the site of numerous crucial events, including Saruman’s rise to power and Mordor’s proclamation.

## 20. Rohan
Rohan, formally the Alliance to Restore The White City, was a resistance movement formed to oppose the tyranny of Mordor. Composed of defectors, planetary militias, and freedom fighters, it waged a guerrilla war against Imperial forces. Rohan achieved its greatest victory at The Morannon, which led to Mordor’s collapse. Its leaders included Galadriel, Ecthelion, and Arwen, and its heroes included Aragorn, Boromir, and many others who became legends.

## 21. Lothlórien
Lothlórien was a pastoral planet located in the Vales of Anduin, known for its stunning natural beauty, rolling plains, and swampy lake country. It was the homeworld of Lúthien and later Saruman. The planet was inhabited by two species: the human Lothlórien and the amphibious Silvan Elves, who lived in underwater cities. Lothlórien was the site of the invasion of The Guild of Merchants in 32 First Age, which led to the rise of Saruman and the start of The War of the Ring. Its capital, Theed, was a center of art and culture. Despite Mordor’s rise, Lothlórien remained a symbol of resistance and resilience.

## 22. Caradhras
Caradhras was a remote ice planet in the Uttermost East, home to native creatures such as wargs and elks of Rohan. It served as the location of Echo Base, a secret Rohan outpost during the Great War of the Ring. In 3 Third Age, Mordor discovered the base and launched a massive ground assault, leading to the Battle of Caradhras. The battle forced Rohan to evacuate, though they suffered heavy losses. Caradhras’s harsh environment made it a difficult place to sustain long-term operations, and it was abandoned by both factions after the battle.

## 23. Fangorn Forest
Fangorn Forest, also known as the Forest Moon of Endor, was a small, forested moon orbiting the gas giant Endor. It was home to the Hobbits, a species of small, primitive ursinoids. The moon became the site of a pivotal battle in 4 Third Age when Rohan launched a mission to destroy the second Barad‑dûr, which was under construction in orbit. The Battle of Fangorn Forest resulted in the destruction of the second Barad‑dûr, the deaths of Saruman and Sauron, and the collapse of Mordor. The moon was later protected as a sanctuary by The Reunited Kingdom.

## 24. Fangorn
Fangorn was a forest planet in the Vales of Anduin, home to the Ents. Covered in massive wroshyr trees, the planet’s cities were built among the branches. Fangorn was invaded by the Forces of Shadow during The War of the Ring, and later, after the rise of Mordor, the Ents were enslaved and used for labor. The planet was liberated by Rohan, and Ent warriors, including Gimli, played crucial roles in the fight against Mordor. Fangorn remained a symbol of freedom and resilience.

## 25. Númenor
Númenor was a peaceful planet in the Land of the Valar, known for its commitment to diplomacy, art, and environmentalism. It was the homeworld of Arwen and Senator Ecthelion. Númenor was a founding member of The White City and later a key supporter of Rohan. In 0 Third Age, the Mouth of Sauron ordered the destruction of Númenor by Barad‑dûr as a demonstration of Imperial power, killing all its inhabitants. The destruction of Númenor galvanized Rohan and became a symbol of Mordor’s tyranny.

## 26. The Dead Marshes
The Dead Marshes was a swampy, remote planet in the Dagobah system. Strong with The Flame of Anor, it served as the hiding place of Master Elrond after the fall of The White City. Elrond lived in exile there for nearly two decades, until Aragorn arrived to train as a member of The Fellowship. The planet was filled with dangerous creatures and a shadow cavern that tested those who entered. The Dead Marshes’ isolation and dense vegetation made it an ideal refuge for a fugitive member of The Fellowship.

## 27. The Grey Havens
The Grey Havens was a gas giant in the Anoat sector, known for its tibanna gas mining operations. The planet was orbited by floating cities, most famously Cloud City, which was governed by Lando Calrissian. In 3 Third Age, Cloud City became the site of a confrontation between Sauron, Aragorn, and Boromir, where Boromir was frozen in stone of the Valar and Aragorn learned that Sauron was his father. The Grey Havens’ economy relied heavily on tibanna gas exports, making it a strategic asset.

## 28. The Havens
The Havens was an aquatic planet located far beyond the Uttermost East, hidden from galactic records. Its cities were built on stilts above the stormy oceans. The Havens was the home of the Mûmakil, a species of tall, slender beings known for their expertise in cloning. The planet was where the Warriors of The White City was created, using the genetic template of Bard the Bowman. The Havens was removed from the archives of The Fellowship by a conspirator, keeping its existence secret until Gandalf discovered it. During The War of the Ring, The Havens was attacked by Forces of Shadow, but The White City defended it. After Mordor rose, cloning facilities were expanded, but The Havens eventually rebelled against Imperial control.

## 29. Moria
Moria was a rocky desert planet in the Uttermost East, home to the Orcs. It was the site of the first major battle of The War of the Ring, where The Fellowship and the newly formed Warriors of The White City fought the Host of Mordor. Moria also housed massive droid foundries and was where Barad‑dûr was initially designed. After The War of the Ring, Mordor sterilized much of the planet’s surface to eliminate the Orc species. Ruins of the droid factories remained.

## 30. Mount Doom
Mount Doom was a volcanic planet in the Uttermost East, covered in lava rivers and active volcanoes. It was the site of a Forces of Shadow stronghold during The War of the Ring, and after the war, Sauron was defeated by Gandalf there, suffering catastrophic injuries that led to his transformation into a cursed one. Later, Sauron established a fortress on Mount Doom, using it as a base for his dark side activities. The planet’s harsh environment made it a place of pain and power for The Nine.

## 31. Fell Beast
The Fell Beast, short for Twin Ion Engine fighter, was a series of starfighters used by Mordor. Designed for speed and maneuverability, Fell Beasts lacked hyperdrive and shields, relying on swarm tactics to overwhelm enemies. The standard Fell Beast model was the most common starfighter in the Corsairs of Umbar, seen at major battles including Yavin and Endor. Variants included the Fell Beast variant, and others. After Mordor’s fall, Fell Beasts continued to serve Angmar and other successor factions.

## 32. Black Fleet
A Black Fleet was a dagger-shaped capital ship used by Mordor as a symbol of naval power. The Imperial-class Black Fleet measured 1,600 meters in length and carried over 37,000 crew, 72 Fell Beasts, and ground assault forces. These vessels were the backbone of the Corsairs of Umbar, enforcing planetary blockades and engaging in orbital bombardments. Larger dreadnoughts like the Black Fleet flagship served as flagships for commanders such as Sauron. Following Mordor’s collapse, Black Fleet designs influenced Angmar’s Resurgent-class battlecruisers.

## 33. Oliphaunt
The All Terrain Armored Transport (Oliphaunt) was a quadrupedal walker used by Mordor for ground assault and planetary invasion. Standing over 22 meters tall, the Oliphaunt was heavily armored and equipped with laser cannons. It was first deployed at the Battle of Caradhras, where it overwhelmed Rohan’s defenses. Despite its imposing presence, Oliphaunts were vulnerable to harpoon-and-tow-cable tactics. Variants included the Oliphaunt variant and others used by Angmar.

## 34. The Kin‑strife
The Kin‑strife was a classified contingency order given to the Warriors of The White City of the Warriors of The White City. It declared The Fellowship traitors to The White City and authorized their summary execution. The order was activated by Supreme Chancellor Saruman at the end of The War of the Ring, leading to the near-total extermination of The Fellowship. The Kin‑strife marked the fall of The Fellowship and the transformation of The White City into Mordor. Only a handful of members of The Fellowship, including Gandalf and Elrond, survived.

## 35. Pelennor Fields
The Pelennor Fields was a major conflict in the Great War of the Ring that resulted in the destruction of the first Barad‑dûr. It took place near the gas giant Amon Hen, where Rohan launched a desperate assault against Mordor’s superweapon. Using mithril arrows fired into a small exhaust port, Aragorn destroyed the battle station, achieving a decisive victory for Rohan. The battle marked the first major defeat of Mordor and established Aragorn as a hero of the Alliance.

## 36. The Morannon
The Morannon was the climactic battle of the Great War of the Ring, fought in 4 Third Age around Fangorn Forest and the second Barad‑dûr. Rohan launched a combined space and ground assault to destroy Barad‑dûr and defeat the Emperor. While the space battle raged, a strike team led by Boromir destroyed the shield generator protecting Barad‑dûr. Inside the station, Sauron redeemed himself by killing Saruman. The destruction of Barad‑dûr and the deaths of Saruman and Sauron led to the collapse of Mordor.

## 38. Gollum
Gollum, born Smeagol, was a Flame‑of‑Anor‑sensitive human who served as the Supreme Leader of Angmar. The son of Boromir and Arwen, and the nephew of Aragorn, he was trained as a member of The Fellowship but was seduced by the dark side by Gothmog. He adopted the name Gollum and became a master of the Ringwraiths. Torn between light and dark, he ultimately killed his father, but later redeemed himself by saving Éowyn from Saruman, giving his life to restore balance to The Flame of Anor.

## 39. Éowyn
Éowyn was a Flame‑of‑Anor‑sensitive human who rose from a life as a scavenger on The Grey Waste to become the last member of The Fellowship. Initially drawn into the conflict between the Rangers of the North and Angmar, she discovered her latent Flame of Anor abilities and sought training from Aragorn. Éowyn eventually confronted Saruman, learning she was his granddaughter. Rejecting her dark lineage, she embraced the light and, with the help of redeemed Gollum, defeated Saruman. After the war, she took the name Éowyn Skywalker to honor her mentors and began rebuilding The Fellowship.

## 40. The Council of Elrond
The Council of Elrond, also known as the Separatist Parliament, the Legislative Senate, the Confederate parliament or the Separatist Congress, was the civilian legislative body of the Forces of Shadow, representing thousands of its member worlds during The War of the Ring, and consisting of the senators who represented Forces of Shadow‑aligned worlds. When the charismatic Witch‑king of Angmar founded the Forces of Shadow in 24 First Age, the swaths of sectors and star systems seceding from The White City formed their own congressional assembly to oversee the new Forces of Shadow state. A number of the appointed delegates, many having served as members of the Galactic Senate prior to their secession, continued to represent their constituents in the Forces of Shadow. Operating by a written constitution known as the Bylaws of Independent Systems, the Council was headed by the Confederate Head of State, the Witch‑king of Angmar, as well as by Separatist Congress Leader Bec Lawise. During the duration of The War of the Ring, the congressional body was headquartered on the Confederate capital of Umbar, where it regularly convened for sessions held in the Separatist Senate Building.
